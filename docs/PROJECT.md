## Domain
The system is meant to assist nonprofit companies, and grant dependent services easier accessibility and matching to federal loans that match their mission. The system simulates the challenge of serving multiple users at once, allowing users to search for grants, processing eligibility matching requests, delivering personalized grant recommendations, and notifying organizations when funding opportunities change or deadlines approach. 
## Scalability Problem
The reason why a scalable distributed webs system is nessasary rest primarily on the fact single server can become a bottleneck as thousands of organizations search, filter, and request eligibility analyses during periods of high demand spexcificly around the change of the fiscal year which is what the majority of grants are based around. This makes caching async processing, load balancing, and resilient service design essential. When services go down NPOs won't be able to find suitable grants, and could potentialy miss important deadlines to aquire the much needed funding.
## Computing for The Common Good
Non-Profits, by definition have to serve a public or social mission. By assisting non-profits to generate funding our project aims to help those who help others. Many small non-profit companies struggle to wade through grants, and determine if it is applicable to their cause, or when they don't have money to pay for grant finding tools. Ensuring nonprofit companies do not have a lack of funds is particularly important as it could either disrupt their services or in the worse case lead to the legal dissolution of the nonprofit. In this way the system can help ensure these non profits are able to continue to provide their services to their communities. I specific example that is fairly close would be the Northfield, MA food pantry, operated out of the town library or in a larger sense the Food Bank of Western Massachusetts. Due to the importance of the work NPOs do reliablity is particularly important and while latency is important for ease of use due to the larger timescale that grants revolve around it clealy is less important compared to ensuing that the NPOs have the most reliable expirience possible.
